Nutrition Basics

Proper nutrition is one of the most important factors influencing a dog's health, well-being, and longevity. Understanding the basics can help dog owners make more informed decisions and look beyond marketing claims.

Did you know?

Dogs require protein and fat as essential macronutrients. While dogs can digest carbohydrates, there is currently no established minimum dietary requirement for carbohydrates in healthy dogs.

Protein

Protein provides the building blocks for muscles, organs, skin, coat, hormones, enzymes, and the immune system. Dogs require specific amino acids that must be supplied through their diet. High-quality animal proteins are generally highly digestible and provide a complete amino acid profile.

Fat

Fat is the most concentrated source of energy in a dog's diet. It provides essential fatty acids and supports skin health, coat quality, brain function, and the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.

Fibre

While fibre is often associated with plant ingredients, dogs may also consume indigestible animal-derived materials such as fur and feathers, which can contribute to digestive function and are part of many natural prey-based diets.

Vitamins & Minerals

Vitamins and minerals are essential for countless biological processes, including bone development, metabolism, immune function, and cellular repair.

Water

Water is often overlooked, yet it is the most important nutrient. Fresh, clean water should always be available.

Nutrition Changes Throughout Life

Puppies, adults, and senior dogs all have different nutritional needs. Age, activity level, body condition, and health status can influence the ideal diet for an individual dog.

Common Feeding Mistakes

  • Overfeeding
  • Too many treats
  • Frequent diet changes
  • Ignoring body condition
  • Following nutrition myths without evidence
